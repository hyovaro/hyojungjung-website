Teaching &
Cultural Work

Teaching acting, choir, and music video; opening and coordinating cultural programmes from pansori to dance; and speaking on Korean culture and film — in Riyadh.

Three Roles

Since 2024, Hyojung has worked at the King Sejong Institute at Prince Sultan University in Riyadh — Saudi Arabia's leading centre for Korean cultural education. Her work there takes three forms: as an instructor she teaches Korean drama and film acting, conducts the KOSA Choir, and runs the music video course; as an organiser and coordinator she opens cultural classes and stages screenings, talks, and performances; and she is an invited panellist on Korean culture and film.

Role 01 — Instructor

What I Teach

Korean drama & film acting, the KOSA Choir, and the music video course — and the stages they reach.

Classes I teach

코사합창단  ·  Conductor

Saudi–Korean Choir

Conducting a 15-member ensemble — from Arirang to BTS — in weekly classes and several concerts a year. The choir focuses on vocal technique, ensemble listening, and the joy of singing Korean together.

Featured by the Sejong Institute Foundation →

케이드라마클럽  ·  Acting

Korean Drama &
Film Acting

Scene study, physical expression, and character work drawn from Korean film and television — including memorable 〈Squid Game〉 sequences — taught in Korean and English, building language competency alongside creative confidence.

뮤직비디오 코스  ·  Music Video

Music Video Course

Students produce their own K-pop-style music videos — choreography, filming, and editing in one creative project. Past projects include ROSÉ's 〈APT.〉 and Kim Won-jun's 〈Show〉.
